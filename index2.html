PROJ-002

Tug of Paw

Market Challenge

During COVID-19, UK pet adoptions surged. As owners returned to work, dogs were left isolated, leading to disruptive behaviours. Existing tug toys all require a human operator — there is no self-automated tug-of-war system on the market. The aim was a mechanically adjustable device capable of independent, prolonged engagement while safely withstanding the dynamic loads of a lunging dog.

Mechanical Solution

The 'Tug of Paw' is a wall-mounted mechanical system on a 12mm En8 steel shaft. A torsional clock spring stores energy when the dog pulls the climbing rope, automatically retracting it on release. Resistance is generated by a mechanical friction brake; a CSK12PP Sprag Clutch ensures the brake only engages when the dog pulls, allowing free retraction.

FEA & Optimisation

FEA was conducted under a 2000N dynamic load (40kg dog lunging at 5m/s). Topological optimisation reduced the brake disc mass by 40% (550g→332g), decreasing moment of inertia by 37% while maintaining a minimum safety factor of 4.6. Shape optimisation on the custom carabiner hook reduced peak stress by 87%.

Specifications

  • Design MethodPahl & Beitz
  • Dynamic Load2000N
  • Primary Shaft12mm En8
  • RetractionTorsional Spring
  • ResistanceFriction Brake
  • DirectionalSprag Clutch

Cost Saving

Scaling shaft from 30mm to 12mm after force calculations reduced bearing and clutch costs by ~17%, reallocated to higher-grade steel housing.
